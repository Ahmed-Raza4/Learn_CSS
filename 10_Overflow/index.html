When working with web layouts, managing content that exceeds the boundaries of its container is essential. The overflow property in CSS is a powerful tool for this purpose. It controls what happens to content that is too large to fit within a given area. By using values such as visible, hidden, scroll, and auto, developers can decide whether to clip the overflowing content, provide scrollbars, or simply allow it to spill out of its container. Understanding how to effectively use overflow is crucial for creating responsive and user-friendly designs, especially when dealing with dynamic content or complex layouts
